# Intro

Welcome to my rendition of yet another vaguely academic personal site (that also sort of looks like the rest of them)!

In all seriousness, I’m Michael:
- (Rising) 2nd-year Computer Science PhD student at Stanford, advised by Chris Ré.
- Previously / also spending wonderful time with: Chelsea Finn (rotation), Percy Liang (rotation), José M. Álvarez (NVIDIA).

I’m broadly interested in making machine learning more usable “in the real world”, primarily w.r.t. robustness, personalization, and collaborative training. I also like learning with less labels.

Before these covid times, I received my A.B. in Statistics and Computer Science at Harvard in 2020, where I’m grateful to have worked with Serena Yeung, Susan Murphy, and Alex D’Amour on computer vision and reinforcement learning with healthcare.
